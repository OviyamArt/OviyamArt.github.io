The video was doing well.

180 views in the first few hours. The numbers were climbing naturally. I uploaded it remotely from my iPhone 13 — added a background track I downloaded from Pixabay, published, and everything looked fine.

Then it stopped.

No error. No notification. No warning in the app. Just silence.

I kept checking YouTube Studio on my phone but couldn't see anything wrong. After 8 hours — when I got home and opened Studio on my desktop PC — I finally spotted it.

Content → Shorts → a small flag next to the video. Restriction. Copyright claim on the audio.

Pixabay markets itself as "100% free for commercial use." What they don't tell you is that many tracks on Pixabay have Content ID claims registered by third parties on YouTube. The license doesn't matter — YouTube's Content ID system only checks if the audio fingerprint matches something in its database.

Mine matched. Distribution stopped immediately.

The fix: replaced the audio directly in YouTube Studio using a track from YouTube's own Audio Library. The flag cleared. But the 180-view momentum was already gone.

Lesson learned the hard way — Pixabay audio is not safe for YouTube. YouTube Audio Library is the only zero-risk source.

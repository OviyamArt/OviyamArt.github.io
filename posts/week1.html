Week 1 — Warm-up first, then the real thing

Warm-up exercises, a 90-minute eye, and the gap between seeing and doing.

May 7, 2026 · pencilsketch · artjourney · comeback

Before drawing anything, the warm-up came first. Not because of a rule — but because ten years away from a pencil teaches you some humility. Circles, ovals, hatching, cross-hatching, pressure control. The basics that used to get skipped. This time, they did not get skipped.

Only after the warm-up came the eye. It took 90 minutes. A decade ago it took 20. The muscle memory is gone — but something more stubborn stayed. The eye for detail. Every mistake was visible exactly as it was being made. The spacing wrong, the iris flat, the lashes mechanical. Knowing what is wrong and being able to fix it are two completely different skills. The first one is there. The second one needs rebuilding.

That gap between seeing and doing is where this journey lives. Every week that gap will close a little. Or at least that is the plan.

This week learned: Start with warm-ups. Circles, hatching, pressure gradients — they wake the hand up before asking it to do something real. The pencil still feels right. The paper still smells the same. Some things do not leave you.

Still struggling with: Eyelash spacing looks mechanical. Every lash the same weight and thickness. Need to vary pressure mid-stroke.

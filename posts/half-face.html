Watch this eye into a half face — can I still do this?

The progression as a sketch grows from a single detailed eye into a realistic half-face portrait. Capturing depth and shadows.

day3portraithalf-face

Starting from a single eye and building outward. The eye is the part that always worked — even after ten years, the eye has a familiar logic. The bridge of the nose is where certainty starts to dissolve.

A half-face is harder than two full eyes. The nose has to relate to the eye above it. The cheekbone has to cast the right shadow. Every element is in conversation with every other element. One wrong proportion and the whole thing reads as wrong, even if the viewer cannot name exactly why.

The shading on this one came closer than expected. Not there yet. But closer. The depth in the eye socket is starting to feel like depth rather than just darker pencil marks in a rough shape.

This week learnedBuilding outward from a strong anchor works. Start with the part you know best — in this case the eye — then let the rest of the face grow from that certainty. Do not start with the outline of the whole face first.

Still struggling withThe transition from the eye to the nose bridge — the value shift is too abrupt. It needs a mid-tone that is currently missing. The jump from light to dark without a gradient in between is what makes it look flat.
